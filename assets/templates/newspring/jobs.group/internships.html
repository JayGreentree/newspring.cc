Position Descriptions

Central Staff

The central team exists to serve the ministries and teams at the campuses, and the campus teams serve the volunteers and guests at their campus directly. Campus teams will lend more to hands on working with people. Central teams will lend more to planning and systems work for the church as a whole.

Guest Services

Guest Services is a ministry made up of many teams including greeting, facility cleaning crew, parking, ushering, and more that are all committed to making a guest's experience at NewSpring excellent. Interns will need to believe that first impressions are important and love creating welcoming environments for every guest at NewSpring.

KidSpring

KidSpring is our children's ministry. Interns will need to have a passion to see children experience Jesus on their level. Interns will be serving and leading volunteers who serve kids in safe, age-appropriate environments where the Bible is taught in a creative and relevant way.

Fuse

Fuse is our student ministry. Interns will need to have a heart to see high school and middle school students grow and fall in love with Jesus daily.

Next Steps

Next Steps is a ministry committed to seeing people grow in their relationship with Jesus one step at a time. Next Steps areas include Baptism, Care, Classes, Financial Coaching, Groups, Resources, and Volunteering.

Production

Production deals with the technical aspects involved with creating an excellent worship service. Interns in this area need to have technical skills as well as attention to detail and a commitment to excellence. Production teams include audio, video, and lighting.

Worship

The goal of the worship internship is that your walk with God would be closer, your calling would be clearer, and your craft would be sharper. You can apply to intern with the central team to get a broad view of what worship looks like at our church, or you can apply for any of our campuses to get a front-lines view of what it takes to make Sundays happen each week. A video audition is required for the worship internship.

Internship FAQ

How do I apply?

Fill out this online application. Be mindful of all deadlines. We cannot accept applications after deadlines have passed.

Will I hear from someone immediately?

You will receive a confirmation email upon submitting your application. We will only call applicants we choose to interview.

Is there an age requirement for the internship?

You must be at least 18 years old.

Are international applicants eligible?

Yes. Other documentation will be needed should you choose to apply.

What is a typical weekly schedule?

It varies per area of ministry, but you can expect a Sunday–Thursday work week with Friday and Saturday off (barring any special events that may take place on those days). That means you will work 4 days during the week (and some evenings when needed). Sundays will involve you being present at your campus' services.

Do I get time off?

Yes, you will get 2 full days off during the week (most likely Friday and Saturday). There are also certain holidays during each semester that the offices are closed. Please inquire about those for the semester for which you apply.

Why don't I see an option to apply for a worship internship on the application?

You may be filling out the general internship application. There is a specific application for the worship internship. You can find it here. 

Do you provide housing?

No. All interns are responsible for securing their own housing.

Is the internship paid?

All internships are unpaid.

How many hours are expected?

We require a minimum time commitment of 25 hours per week.

What is the length of the internship?

Each internship semester is 12-15 weeks. Spring internships run from January to May, Summer internships run from May to August, and Fall internships run from August to December.
